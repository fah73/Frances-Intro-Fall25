Field or Career

As a student double majoring in Public and Professional Writing and Digital Narrative and Interactive Design, I find that one of the most important things to me is communication. Since I was a kid, I've loved reading and writing and as I got older I developed a sense of justice surrounding the accessability and communication of complicated topics. In many ways, I can trace this pattern back to the many amazing teachers I had who went the extra mile to help me understand every topic I struggled with. These teachers all helped me break the habit of thinking I just wasn't smart enough. From there, I developed my own passion for wanting to write and communicate topics between individuals or to large audiences.

Family and Home

A collection of some of the most important people and places in my life!

I consider family to be anyone that I love. My immediate family, my mom and sister, have always been my biggest cheerleaders. When I decided to commute an hour to and from a performing arts high school, they put in the hours driving me there and back before I could drive myself and showing up for me at every show or reading. They are my home. I feel very lucky that my family has extended to include the friends I've grown to love here at Pitt. They're my home away from home! They celebrate the small wins with me, like getting into a class I was sure would fill up, and they sit in the lows with me. These people I've met here and consider my family are the same ones who encourage me to take advantage of every opportunity and dream a little bigger than I have before.

Since I was little, I've viewed media as an awesome creative outlet. I was the kid posting badly edited iMovies on Youtube, and I still consume and create a lot of artistic media. I distinctly remember watching the Katy Perry's Firework music video on repeat and loved the visual storytelling of it all. Now, I try to stay involved in making new media - like working on film sets when possible - and I often catch myself "remaking" media. I will constantly be refrencing popular trends or Tiktoks among frineds or with my sister, and sometimes it's one of the first ways I connect with a new friend. While I'm sure my screen time is absurd, the media that I spend time consuming certainly impact my social life and creative spark.

Community

The majority of my community is acutal. For someone who spends so much time on social media and my phone, I don't often engage with the content aside from viewing it, but as I mentioned this media often sparks conversation and connection within my physical communities. Pathfinders, the tour guide association at Pitt, brought me the largest community I am physically a part of. To get a little philosophical with it, I also find that there is a huge community among those who read. Most recently, I re-read Jane Austen's Pride and Prejudice. The forward for the copy was written by a critic who shared their thoughts on the story, and I inevitably ended up sharing many of the same sentiments and thinking about their thoughts as I finished the novel.

School

School for me, before college, was designed for creative students. Lincoln Park was a performing arts school where the goal was creation of original material. The expectations I faced were not grade based, but production based. Write a good play this semester, and you'll be rewarded an A. Put as much effort into it as you phyiscally can. I know this line of thinking does not quite align with most other high schools, but I genuinley believe that it prepared me well for where I am in college and for the future. I have known since my freshman year of high school how to sit down and put time and effort into a genuine piece of writing instead of following rubrics and precise guidelines in the hope of an A.

Me, technology, AI, and futures

Once I got to college, I strayed from creative writing and focused myself in on professional written communications. I enjoy learning about technical topics, like technical writing, and one of my favorite classes I've taken at Pitt was Writing Machines. I have found that AI can be a helpful tool, and I believe it's going to be essential that writers and communicators in the future understand how to use the technology without sacrificing their personal talents and thoughts.
